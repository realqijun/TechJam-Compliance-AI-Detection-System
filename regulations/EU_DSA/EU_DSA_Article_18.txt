Statement:

Hosting providers must promptly inform law enforcement or judicial authorities if they become aware of information suggesting a criminal offence involving threats to life or safety:

Has taken place,

Is taking place, or

Is likely to take place.

Providers must give all relevant information available.

If the relevant Member State cannot be identified with certainty, the provider must notify:

Authorities in the Member State where it is established, or

Where its legal representative resides, or

Europol (or both).

Context:

Defines the “concerned Member State” as:

Where the offence occurred or is likely to occur,

Where the suspected offender resides or is located, or

Where the victim resides or is located.

Ensures rapid communication between providers and authorities to protect human life and safety.

Strengthens cooperation with EU-wide law enforcement (Europol) in cross-border or uncertain cases.
