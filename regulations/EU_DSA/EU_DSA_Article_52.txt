Statement:

Member States must establish penalty rules for infringements, ensuring penalties are effective, proportionate, and dissuasive, with specific limits on fines and periodic penalty payments.

Context:

Penalty rules: Member States are required to define penalties for infringements of this Regulation and ensure their implementation in accordance with Article 51.

Proportionality: Penalties must be effective, proportionate, and dissuasive, and Member States must notify the Commission of the rules and any subsequent amendments.

Fines: The maximum fine for non-compliance with the Regulation’s obligations is 6% of the provider’s annual worldwide turnover. For supplying incorrect or misleading information, failure to reply or rectify information, and non-compliance with inspections, the maximum fine is 1% of the provider's income or turnover.

Periodic penalty payments: The maximum amount for a periodic penalty payment is 5% of the average daily worldwide turnover or income, calculated per day from the date specified in the decision.
